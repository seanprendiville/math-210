To save on brackets (at the cost of multiple subscripts) we may also write "let \((a_{k_n})_{n =1}^\infty\) be a subsequence of \((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty\)" to mean that the function \(\N \to \N\) given by \(n \mapsto k_n\) is strictly increasing, so that
\begin{equation*} k_1 \lt k_2 \lt k_3 \lt \dots \end{equation*}
