We prove the result for the sum, the remaining arguments being similar (and left to the reader). By definition of function limits (Definition 5.11) and definition of continuity (Definition 5.13), we are required to show that if \((a_n)_n\) is a sequence in \(D\) with \(a_n\to x_0\) then \(f(a_n)+g(a_n)\to f(x_0)+g(x_0)\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\) Since both \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(x_0\text{,}\) we have \(f(a_n)\to f(x_0)\) and \(g(a_n)\to g(x_0)\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\) Thus by the addition rule for sequential limits (Proposition 2.8) we have
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to \infty} \brac{f(a_n)+g(a_n)} = \lim_{n\to \infty} f(a_n)+\lim_{n\to \infty} g(a_n) = f(x_0) + g(x_0)\text{,} \end{equation*}
as required.
