Proposition 5.19. Epsilon-delta characterisation of continuity.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) be a real valued function on a real domain \(D \subset \R\) and let \(x_0\in D\text{.}\) Then \(f\) is continuous at \(x_0\) if and only if
\begin{align} (\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta=\delta(x_0,\eps) \gt 0)\amp(\forall x \in D)\notag\\ \amp \bigsqbrac{\ |x-x_0| \leq \delta \implies |f(x)-f(x_0)| \leq \eps\ }.\tag{5.5} \end{align}
Proof.
