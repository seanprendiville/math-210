By the extreme value theorem (Theorem 6.20), there exists \(x_1,x_2 \in [a,b]\) which are minima and maxima (respectively) for \(f\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\) In particular \(x_1\) is a local maximum and \(x_2\) is a local minimum. If \(x_1 \in (a,b)\) or \(x_2 \in (a,b)\text{,}\) then it follows that \(f'(x_1) = 0\) or \(f'(x_2) = 0\) since local minima/maxima have zero derivative at points of differentiability (Proposition 7.8). We may therefore assume that \(\set{x_1, x_2} \subset \set{a,b}\text{.}\) Hence \(f(x_1) = f(x_2)\text{.}\) For any \(x \in [a,b]\text{,}\) we have that \(f(x)\) is between \(f(x_1)\) and \(f(x_2)\text{.}\) Since \(f(x_1) = f(x_2)\text{,}\) it follows that \(f\) is constant on \([a,b]\text{.}\) It then follows from Example 6.25 that \(f'(x)= 0\) at every \(x \in (a,b)\text{,}\) e.g. for \(x := \frac{a+b}{2}\text{.}\)
