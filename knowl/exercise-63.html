- 7.22. Exam-style self-test question for workshop.

Let \(f : [a,b] \to \R\) be an increasing and continuous function with \(f(a) \leq 0 \leq f(b)\text{.}\) The purpose of this question is to prove a special case of the intermediate value theorem for \(f\text{.}\)
(a)
State the conclusion of the intermediate value theorem for \(f\text{.}\)
Solution.
This is Theorem 6.11.
(b)
Define the set
\begin{equation} X_0:= \set{x\in [a,b] : f(x)\leq 0}\text{.}\tag{7.12} \end{equation}
Justify why \(\sup X_0\) exists.
Solution.
This is an instance of Theorem 1.15.
(c)
Write
\begin{equation} x_0 := \sup \set{x\in [a,b] : f(x)\leq 0}\text{.}\tag{7.13} \end{equation}
Justify why the assumption \(f(x_0) \gt 0\) implies that there exists \(x_1 \lt x_0\) with \(f(x_1)\gt 0\text{,}\) and why this contradicts the definition of \(x_0\text{.}\)
Solution.
This can be adapted from the proof of Theorem 6.11.
(d)
Defining \(x_0\) as in (7.13), justify why the assumption \(f(x_0) \lt 0\) implies that there exists \(x_2 \gt x_0\) with \(f(x_2)\leq 0\text{,}\) and why this contradicts the definition of \(x_0\text{.}\) Hence explain why you may conclude that \(f(x_0) = 0\text{.}\)
Solution.
This can be adapted from the proof of Theorem 6.11.
