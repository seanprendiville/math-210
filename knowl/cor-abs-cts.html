Proposition 6.8.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) with \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\) is continuous at \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\) Then \(|f|\) is continuous at \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\)
Proof.
This follows from the fact that continuity is preserved under composition of functions (Proposition 6.7), and the fact that the absolute value function \(x\mapsto |x|\) is continuous on \(\R\) (Example 5.25).
