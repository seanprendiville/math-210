We first prove the "\(\implies\)" direction. Let \((a_n)_n\) be a sequence in \((a,b)\setminus\set{x_0}\) such that \(a_n \to x_0\) as \(n \to \infty\text{.}\) We are required to prove (6.4), assuming (6.3). Fix \(\eps \gt0\text{.}\) Then by (6.3) there exists \(\delta = \delta(\eps) \gt 0\) such that if \(x \in (a,b)\) and \(0\lt |x-x_0| \leq \delta\) then

\begin{equation*} \abs{\frac{f(x)-f(x_0)}{x-x_0} - y}\leq \eps\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(a_n \to x_0\) there exists \(N = N(\delta)\) such that for all \(n\geq N\) we have \(0\lt |a_n - x_0| \leq \delta\text{.}\) Hence if \(n \geq N(\delta)\) then

\begin{equation*} \abs{\frac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}{a_n-x_0} - y}\leq \eps\text{.} \end{equation*}

This establishes (6.4).
