Proof
Since \(a_n \to \infty\text{,}\) the sequence is eventually positive, in that there is some \(N_1\) such that if \(n\geq N_1\) then \(a_n\gt 0\text{.}\) If \(N\) denotes the quantity appearing in (3.4), then for all \(n\geq \max\set{N_1, N}\) we have \(a_nb_n \geq a_n\delta \text{.}\) By the scaling rule (Proposition 3.8) we have \(a_n\delta \to \infty\text{,}\) so by the (one-sided) sandwich rule (Proposition 3.6) we have \(a_nb_n\to \infty\text{.}\)
