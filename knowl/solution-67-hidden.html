Proposition 6.23. Sequential characterisation of differentiability.
Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\) and \(x_0 \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then \(f\) is differentiable at \(x_0\) with derivative \(y\) if and only if for any sequence \((a_n)_n\) in \((a,b)\setminus\set{x_0}\) with \(a_n\to x_0\) we have
\begin{equation} \frac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}{a_n-x_0} \to y \text{ as } n \to \infty\text{.}\tag{6.4} \end{equation}
Proof.
