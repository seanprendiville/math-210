Example 6.27. The reciprocal function is differentiable.
Let \(f : \R\setminus\set{0} \to \R\) be given by \(f(x):=1/x\) for all \(x \in \R\setminus\set{0}\text{.}\) Then for \(h\neq 0\) and \(x \in \R\setminus\set{0}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{\recip{x+h}-\recip{x}}{h} = -\recip{(x+h)x} \to -\recip{x^2} \quad \text{as} \quad h \to 0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(f\) is differentiable on \(\R\setminus\set{0}\text{.}\) Moreover \(f'(x) =-1/x^2\) on \(\R\setminus\set{0}\text{.}\)
