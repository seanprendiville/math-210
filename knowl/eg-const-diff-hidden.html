Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\) is constant. In other words, there exists \(c \in \R\) such that for all \(x \in (a,b)\) we have \(f(x) = c\text{.}\) Then for \(h\neq 0\) and \(x \in (a,b)\) we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{c-c}{h} = 0 \to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad h \to 0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(f\) is differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) Moreover \(f' = 0\) on \((a,b)\text{.}\)
