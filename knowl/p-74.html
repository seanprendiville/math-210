Next we prove the "\(\implies\)" direction. To do so we prove the contrapositive. Hence, suppose that the negation of (5.5) holds:

\begin{align*} (\exists \eps \gt 0)(\forall \delta \gt 0)\amp(\exists x= x(\delta) \in D)\\ \amp \bigsqbrac{\ |x-x_0| \leq \delta\quad \text{and}\quad |f(x)-f(x_0)| \gt \eps\ }. \end{align*}

Taking \(\delta = 1/n\) in the above negation, set \(a_1:= x(1)\text{,}\) \(a_2:=x(1/2)\text{,}\) \(a_3 := x(1/3)\text{,}\) etc. Then \(a_n \in D\text{,}\) \(|a_n - x_0| \leq 1/n\) and \(|f(a_n)-f(x_0)| \geq \eps\text{.}\) Hence \((a_n)_n\) is a sequence in \(D\) with \(a_n \to x_0\) and \(f(a_n) \not\to f(x_0)\) (as required).
