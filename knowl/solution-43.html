Proposition 3.20. Limits are preserved on passing to subsequences.
Let \((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) be a real sequence with \(a_n \to a\text{.}\) Suppose that \((k_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) is a sequence of positive integers (not necessarily strictly increasing) such that \(k_n \to \infty\text{.}\) Then \(a_{k_n} \to a\) as \(n \to \infty\text{.}\) In particular, any subsequence \((a_{k_n})_{n =1}^\infty\) of \((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) tends to the same limit.
Proof.
Example 3.21. Sequences may not have the same limit as their subsequences.
