By the epsilon-delta definition of continuity (see Proposition 5.19) we are required to verify that
\begin{align} (\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta_1 = \delta_1(x_0,\eps) \gt 0)\amp(\forall x \in (a,b))\notag\\ \amp \bigsqbrac{\ |x-x_0| \leq \delta_1 \implies |f(x)-f(x_0)| \leq \eps\ }.\tag{6.5} \end{align}
By the definition of differentiability (see Definition 6.22) we have that
\begin{align*} (\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta_2 = \delta_2(x_0,\eps) \gt 0)\amp (\forall x \in (a,b))\\ \sqbrac{\ 0\lt |x-x_0| \leq \delta_2 \implies \abs{\frac{f(x)-f(x_0)}{x-x_0}-f'(x_0)} \leq \eps\ }. \end{align*}
In particular, if \(x \in (a,b) \) and \(0\lt|x-x_0| \leq \delta_2\) then
\begin{equation*} \abs{f(x)-f(x_0)} \leq \eps \abs{x-x_0} + \abs{f'(x_0)}|x-x_0|. \end{equation*}
Let us assume that \(f'(x_0) \neq 0\text{,}\) the remaining case being simpler. We therefore obtain (6.5) on taking
\begin{equation*} \delta_1(\eps) := \min\set{\delta_2(\eps), \recip{2}, \frac{\eps}{2\abs{f'(x_0)}}}. \end{equation*}
