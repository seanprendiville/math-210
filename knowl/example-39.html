Example 10.16.
Let \(q_1, q_2, \dots\) be an enumeration 4  of the rationals in \([0,1]\text{.}\) The sequence of functions \(f_n := 1_{\set{q_1, \dots, q_n}} : [0,1] \to \R\) converges pointwise to \(f := 1_{\Q \cap [0,1]}\) on \([0,1]\text{.}\) Each \(f_n\) is Riemann integrable on \([0,1]\) by [cross-reference to target(s) "eg-indicator-point" missing or not unique] and linearity of the integral (Theorem 9.13). Moreover \(\int_0^1 f_n = 0\text{.}\) Yet by Example 10.3, the function \(f= 1_{\Q \cap [0,1]}\) is not Riemann integrable on \([0,1]\text{.}\) This example shows that, unlike uniform convergence, pointwise convergence is not sufficient to ensure Riemann integrability of the limit.
