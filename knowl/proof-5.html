Proof
3 is an upper bound for \(\set{1,2,3}\text{.}\) We must show it is the least upper bound. Suppose that \(M\) is another upper bound for \(\set{1,2,3}\text{.}\) Then by definition of being an upper bound and the fact that 3 lies in our set, we have that \(3 \leq M\text{.}\) Thus 3 is smaller than any other upper bound, thereby satisfies the definition (DefinitionÂ 1.6) of being a supremum.
