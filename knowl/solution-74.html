Solution 5.13.c.1.

Proposition 5.21. Non-zero point of continuity implies non-zero on a neighbourhood.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) be a real-valued function on a real domain \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\) is continuous at \(x_0 \in D\) and \(f(x_0)\gt 0\text{.}\) Then there exists \(\delta \gt 0\) such that for all \(x \in D\cap(x_0-\delta, x_0+\delta)\) we have \(f(x) \gt 0 \text{.}\)
Proof.
Take \(\eps := \trecip{2} f(x_0)\) in (5.4). Then by the triangle inequality, for \(x \in D\) satisfying \(|x-x_0| \leq \delta = \delta(\eps)\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(x) = f(x_0) + f(x) - f(x_0) \geq f(x_0)- |f(x)-f(x_0)| \geq f(x_0) - \eps = \trecip{2} f(x_0) \gt 0. \end{equation*}
