Proposition 7.2. Algebra of differentiation.
Let \(f,g: (a,b)\to \R\) be differentiable at \(x_0 \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then the sum \(f+g\) and product \(f\cdot g\) are differentiable at \(x_0\text{.}\) Moreover, their derivatives satisfy the identities
\begin{align*} (f+g)'(x_0) \amp = f'(x_0) + g'(x_0), \amp \text{(addition rule)}\\ (f\cdot g)'(x_0) \amp = f'(x_0)g(x_0) + f(x_0)g'(x_0).\amp \text{(product rule)} \end{align*}
