- 5.26. Exam-style self-test question for workshop.

(a)
Define the series \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) and what it means for this series to exist and converge.
Solution.
This is a version of - 4.1.
(b)
Suppose that \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n| \neq \infty\text{.}\) Prove that there exists \(N \in \N\) such that for all \(M \gt N\) we have
\begin{equation*} |a_{N+1}|+|a_{N+2}| + \dots + |a_M| \leq \frac{1}{1000}. \end{equation*}
Solution.
This is a special case of the first part of the proof of Task 4.9.b.
(c)
Justify why \(|a_{1}+a_{2}+a_{3}| \leq |a_{1}|+|a_{2}|+|a_{3}|\text{,}\) and more generally why for all \(M \gt N\) we have
\begin{equation*} \abs{\sum_{n=1}^M a_n - \sum_{n=1}^N a_n}\leq |a_{N+1}|+|a_{N+2}|+\dots +|a_{M}|\text{.} \end{equation*}
Solution.
This is the triangle inequality, iterated.
(d)
Using the previous parts, prove that if \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n| \neq \infty\) then the series \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) converges.
Solution.
This is the second half of the proof of Task 4.9.b.
