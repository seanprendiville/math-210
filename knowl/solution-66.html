Definition 6.22. Differentiable.
Let \(f : (a,b)\to \R\) and \(x_0 \in (a,b)\text{.}\) We say that \(f\) is differentiable at \(x_0\) if
\begin{align} (\exists y \in \R)(\forall \eps \gt 0)\amp(\exists \delta\gt 0)(\forall x \in (a,b))\notag\\ \amp\sqbrac{0\lt |x-x_0| \leq \delta \implies \abs{\frac{f(x)-f(x_0)}{x-x_0}-y} \leq \eps}.\tag{6.3} \end{align}
Should such a value of \(y\) exist, one can check that it is unique, and we call it the derivative of \(f\) at \(x_0\text{,}\) denoted \(f'(x_0)\text{.}\)
