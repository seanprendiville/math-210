Definition 5.2. Power series and radius of convergence.
Given \(x \in \R\text{,}\) the power series associated to the sequence \((a_n)_{n=0}^\infty\) is the series
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n, \end{equation*}
provided that this limit exists.
Example 5.3. The geometric power series.
The power series associated to the constant sequence \(a_n :=1\) is
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty x^n. \end{equation*}
This converges absolutely (and is equal to \((1-x)^{-1}\)) when \(|x|\lt 1\text{.}\) It does not converge when \(|x| \geq 1\text{.}\)
