Theorem 4.25. Absolute convergence implies re-arrangement invariant.
Let \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) be an absolutely convergent series. Let \(\sigma : \N \to \N\) be a bijection. Then \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_{\sigma(n)}\) is absolutely convergent and
\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_{\sigma(n)} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_{n}.\tag{4.4} \end{equation}
Proof (NON-EXAMINABLE).
