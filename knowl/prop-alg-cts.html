Proposition 6.2. Algebra of continuity.

Let \(f, g : D \to \R\) with \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) Let \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\) Then the sum \(f+g\) and product \(f\cdot g\) are both continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\)

If in addition \(g(x_0) \neq 0\text{,}\) then the quotient \(f/g\) is continuous at \(x_0\) (the quotient may not be defined on all of \(D\text{,}\) but it is defined on \(D \cap (x_0-\delta, x_0+\delta)\) for some \(\delta \gt 0\text{,}\) see PropositionÂ 5.20).
