Definition 6.3. Polynomial.
Let \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) We say that a function \(f : D \to \R\) is a polynomial with real coefficients if there exist \(c_0, c_1, \dots, c_n \in \R\) such that for all \(x \in D\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(x) = c_0 + c_1 x + \dots + c_n x^n. \end{equation*}
We call \(n\) the degree of the polynomial.
