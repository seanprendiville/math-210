Example 5.22. Constant functions are continuous.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) with \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\) is constant. In other words, there exists \(c \in \R\) such that for all \(x \in D\) we have \(f(x) = c\text{.}\) Then for any sequence \((a_n)_n\) in \(D\) we have \(f(a_n) \to c\text{,}\) by ExampleÂ 1.25. Hence \(f\) is continuous on \(D\text{.}\)
