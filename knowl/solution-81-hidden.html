Theorem 7.13.
Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\) be a function which is \(n\)-times differentiable and let \(x_0, x \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then there exists \(x_n\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that

Remark 7.14. Taylor with improved remainder.
With a slightly longer proof, one can actually improve our statement of Taylor's theorem to the following.
Theorem 7.15. Strong Taylor (non-examinable).
Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\) be a function which is \(n\)-times differentiable and let \(x_0, x \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then there exists \(x_n\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that
\begin{align*} f(x) = f(x_0) + (x-x_0)f'(x_0) + \dots + \amp \trecip{(n-1)!} (x-x_0)^{n-1} f^{(n-1)}(x_0)\\ \amp + \trecip{n!}(x-x_0)^nf^{(n)}(x_n). \end{align*}
