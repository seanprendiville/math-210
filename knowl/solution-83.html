Theorem 7.19. Power series are infinitely differentiable within radius of convergence.
Let \((a_n)_{n=0}^\infty\) be a sequence of real numbers. Let \(R \in [0, \infty]\) denote the radius of convergence (see TheoremÂ 5.5) of the corresponding power series:
\begin{equation} f(x) := \sum_{n =0}^\infty a_n x^n.\tag{7.11} \end{equation}
Then for any \(m \in \Z_{\geq0}\) the function \(f : (-R, R) \to \R\) given by (7.11) is \(m\)-times differentiable. Moreover, for any \(x_0 \in (-R, R)\) we have the formula
\begin{equation} f^{(m)}(x_0) = \sum_{n = m}^\infty n(n-1)\dotsm (n-m+1)a_n x_0^{n-m}\tag{7.12} \end{equation}
where the right-hand side of (7.12) is absolutely convergent.
Proof.
