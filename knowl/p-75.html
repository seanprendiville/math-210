In this week's workshop you will be asked to write-up (under exam conditions) two of the following.

  1. This question is a variant of Task 5.6.a. Without assuming convergence of the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{n!}\text{,}\) justify why we have

    \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{\floor{n/2}!} \lt \infty. \end{equation*}

  2. This question is a variant of Task 5.12.b, and as such, you are not permitted to assume the equivalence of the epsilon-delta characterisation of continuity. Let \(f:\R \to \R\) and suppose that

    \begin{equation*} (\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta=\delta(\eps) \gt 0)(\forall x \in \R) \bigsqbrac{\ |x| \leq \delta \implies |f(x)| \leq \eps\ }. \end{equation*}
    Prove that \(f(1/n) \to 0\) as \(n \to \infty\) by verifying the definition of what it means for a sequence to converge to 0.

  3. This question is a variant of Task 5.12.c. Let \(f:\R \to \R\) be continuous and suppose that \(f(0) \gt 0\text{.}\) Prove that there exists \(\delta \gt 0\) such that

    \begin{equation*} \inf_{|x| \lt \delta} f(x) \gt 0. \end{equation*}
