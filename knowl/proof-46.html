Proof
We verify both identities using the sequential characterisation of differentiability (Proposition 6.23) together with the addition and product rules for sequence limits (Proposition 2.8 and Proposition 2.10). To this end, let \((a_n)_n\) be a sequence in \((a,b)\setminus\set{x_0}\) with \(a_n \to x_0\text{.}\) Then the addition rule follows from the addition rule for sequence limits, since
\begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \amp \frac{(f+g)(a_n)-(f+g)(x_0)}{a_n-x_0}\\ \amp = \lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}{a_n-x_0}+ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}{x-x_0}. \end{align*}
The product rule uses the identity
\begin{align*} \amp f(a_n)g(a_n) - f(x_0)g(x_0) = \sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}g(a_n) + f(x_0)\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}\\ \amp =\sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)} + \sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}g(x_0)+ f(x_0)\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}. \end{align*}
By the multiplication rule for sequence limits (Proposition 2.10), we have
\begin{gather*} \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{\sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}g(x_0)}{a_n-x_0} = f'(x_0)g(x_0),\\ \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{f(x_0)\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}}{a_n-x_0} = f(x_0)g'(x_0). \end{gather*}
Similarly
\begin{align*} \amp \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}}{a_n-x_0}\\ \amp = \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\sqbrac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}}{a_n-x_0}\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\sqbrac{g(a_n)-g(x_0)}}{a_n-x_0}\lim_{n\to\infty}(a_n-x_0)\\ \amp = f'(x_0)\cdot g'(x_0)\cdot 0 = 0. \end{align*}
