Proof
We prove the case in which \(y\lt \infty\) and leave the infinite case to the reader.
Take \(\eps := \trecip{2} y\) in (5.4). Then by the triangle inequality, for \(x \in D\) satisfying \(|x-x_0| \leq \delta = \delta(\eps)\) we have
\begin{align*} f(x) = y + f(x) - y \geq y- |f(x)-y| \amp \geq y - \eps\\ \amp = \trecip{2} y \gt 0. \end{align*}
