- 5.25. Positivity spreads around a positive limit.

State and prove how a function which has a positive limit inherits positivity on a neighbourhood of that point.
Solution.
Proposition 5.26. Non-zero limit implies non-zero on a neighbourhood.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) be a real-valued function on a real domain \(D \subset \R\) and let \(x_0 \in \R\text{.}\) Suppose that there exists \(y\gt 0\) such that
\begin{equation*} f(x)\to y\quad\text{as}\quad x\to x_0 \quad\text{through}\quad D. \end{equation*}
Then there exists \(\delta \gt 0\) such that for all \(x \in D\cap[x_0-\delta, x_0+\delta]\) we have \(f(x) \gt 0 \text{.}\)
Proof.
We prove the case in which \(y\lt \infty\) and leave the infinite case to the reader.
Take \(\eps := \trecip{2} y\) in (5.4). Then by the triangle inequality, for \(x \in D\) satisfying \(|x-x_0| \leq \delta = \delta(\eps)\) we have
\begin{align*} f(x) = y + f(x) - y \geq y- |f(x)-y| \amp \geq y - \eps\\ \amp = \trecip{2} y \gt 0. \end{align*}
