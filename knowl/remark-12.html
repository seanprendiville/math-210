Remark 6.7. Uniform continuity implies continuity.
It is important to compare the definition of uniform continuity (6.2) with that of continuity (5.4), the latter being:
\begin{align} (\forall x_0 \in D)(\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta = \delta(x_0,\eps) \gt 0)\amp (\forall x \in D)\notag\\ \bigsqbrac{\ |x-x_0| \leq \delta \implies |f(x)-f(x_0)| \leq \eps\ }.\tag{6.3} \end{align}
The only difference is the order of the quantifiers: for continuity (6.3) the parameter \(\delta= \delta(x_0, \eps)\) is allowed to depend on the point of continuity \(x_0\text{,}\) however for uniformity continuity (6.2) the same \(\delta = \delta(\eps)\) has to work for all points \(x_0\) in the domain \(D\text{.}\) If \(f\) is uniformly continuous then it is therefore continuous, on taking \(\delta(x_0, \eps) := \delta(\eps)\) for any \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\) However, the converse 2  is not always true, as the next example illustrates.
