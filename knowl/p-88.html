With the above facts on absolute convergence established, we are able to prove the \(m=1\) case of (7.12). By linearity of series (Proposition 4.5), for \(x \in [-r,r]\) with \(x\neq x_0\) we have

\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x) - f(x_0)}{x-x_0} - \sum_{n=1}^\infty na_n x_0^{n-1} = \recip{x-x_0}\sum_{n=2}^\infty a_n \sqbrac{x^n-x_0^n-(x-x_0)nx_0^{n-1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

By Taylor's theorem (Theorem 7.13), for each \(n\in \N\) there exists \(x_n\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that

\begin{equation*} \abs{x^n-x_0^n-(x-x_0)nx_0^{n-1}} \leq n(n-1)|x-x_0|^2|x_n|^{n-2}\leq n^2|x-x_0|^2r^{n-2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore

\begin{equation*} \abs{\frac{f(x) - f(x_0)}{x-x_0} - \sum_{n=1}^\infty na_n x_0^{n-1}} \leq \abs{x-x_0}\sum_{n=2}^\infty n^2|a_n|r^{n-2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

It follows that Definition 6.22 is satisfied on taking

\begin{equation*} \delta(x_0, \eps) := \min\set{\frac{\eps}{\sum_{n=2}^\infty n^2|a_n|r^{n-2}}, r-|x_0|} \gt 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
