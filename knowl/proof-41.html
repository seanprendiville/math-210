Proof (NON-EXAMINABLE).
Let us first prove (4.4), and later deduce absolute convergence of the re-arranged series. We would like to show that the partial sums \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^N a_{\sigma(n)}\) converge to \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\text{.}\) By linearity of series (Proposition 4.13) we have
\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n- \sum_{n=1}^N a_{\sigma(n)} = \sum_{\substack{n=1\\ n\neq \sigma(1),\dots, \sigma(N)}}^\infty a_n.\tag{4.5} \end{equation}
The idea is to show that
\begin{equation} (\forall M \in \N) (\exists N_0 \in \N)(\forall N \geq N_0)\bigsqbrac{ \set{1,2,\dots, M} \subset \set{\sigma(1), \dots ,\sigma(N)}}.\tag{4.6} \end{equation}
If this holds, then combining (4.5) and (4.6), together with the infinite triangle inequality (Proposition 4.11), we deduce that for all \(N \geq N_0\) we have
\begin{equation*} \abs{\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n- \sum_{n=1}^N a_{\sigma(n)}} \leq \sum_{n=M}^\infty |a_n|,% \quad \to 0 \quad \text{ as } \quad M \to \infty. \end{equation*}
and we can make the latter arbitrarily small by taking \(M\) large. To make this idea rigorous, we first prove (4.6). Since \(\sigma : \N\to \N\) is a bijection, we may take its inverse, so set
\begin{equation*} N_0 = N_0(M) := \max\set{\sigma^{-1}(1), \dots, \sigma^{-1}(M)}. \end{equation*}
If \(1 \leq m \leq M\) then \(m = \sigma(\sigma^{-1}(m))\) with \(\sigma^{-1}(m) \in \set{1,\dots, N_0}\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} \set{1, \dots,M} \subset \set{\sigma(1), \dots, \sigma(N_0)} , \end{equation*}
which proves (4.6). Next let us use (4.6) to show that \(\sum_{n=1}^N a_{\sigma(n)}\) converges to \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\text{.}\) Since \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n| \lt \infty\text{,}\) for any \(\eps \gt 0\) there exists \(M = M(\eps)\) such that
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=M}^\infty |a_n| = \abs{\sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n| - \sum_{n=1}^{M-1} |a_n|} \leq \eps. \end{equation*}
Using (4.5) and (4.6), for \(N \geq N_0(M)\) we deduce that
\begin{equation*} \abs{\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n - \sum_{n=1}^N a_{\sigma(n)}} \leq \sum_{\substack{n=1\\ n\neq \sigma(1),\dots, \sigma(N)}}^\infty |a_n| \leq \sum_{n=M}^\infty |a_{n}| \leq \eps. \end{equation*}
Since \(\eps > 0\) is arbitrary, we deduce (4.4). Absolute convergence of the re-arranged series follows by applying (4.4) to the sequence \((|a_n|)_{n=1}^\infty\text{,}\) to yield
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_{\sigma(n)}| = \sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n| \lt \infty. \end{equation*}
