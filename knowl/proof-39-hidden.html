We first observe that
\begin{equation*} \max\set{f, 0} = \frac{ f+ |f|}{2}. \end{equation*}
The latter function is continuous at \(x_0\) on combining Proposition 6.2 and Proposition 6.8. Next observe that
\begin{equation*} \max\set{f,g}= \max\set{f-g,0} + g. \end{equation*}
The latter function is continuous at \(x_0\) on combining our previous observation with the fact that the sum of two continuous functions is continuous (Proposition 6.2). The argument for \(\min\) is similar.
