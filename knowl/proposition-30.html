Proposition 6.9. Min/max of two continuous functions is continuous.
Let \(f, g: D \to \R\) be real valued functions on a real domain \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) Suppose that both \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\) Then both \(\min\set{f,g}\) and \(\max\set{f,g}\) are continuous at \(x_0 \in D\text{.}\)
