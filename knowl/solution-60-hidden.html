Definition 6.11. Uniformly continuous.
Let \(f : D \to \R\) be a real valued function on a real domain \(D \subset \R\text{.}\) We say that \(f\) is uniformly continuous on \(D\) if
\begin{align} (\forall \eps \gt 0)(\exists \delta = \delta(\eps) \gt 0)\amp (\forall x_1,x_2 \in D)\notag\\ \amp\bigsqbrac{\ |x_1-x_2| \leq \delta \implies |f(x_1)-f(x_2)| \leq \eps\ }.\tag{6.1} \end{align}
