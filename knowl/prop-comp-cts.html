Proposition 6.7. Composition preserves continuity.
Let \(f_1 : D_1 \to D_2\) with \(D_1, D_2 \subset \R\text{.}\) Let \(f_2 : D_2 \to \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f_1\) is continuous at \(x_0 \in D_1\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f_2\) is continuous at \(f_1(x_0)\text{.}\) Then the composition 1 \(f_2 \circ f_1\) is continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\)
