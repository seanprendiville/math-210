Proposition 6.7. Composition preserves continuity.
Let \(f_1 : D_1 \to D_2\) with \(D_1, D_2 \subset \R\text{.}\) Let \(f_2 : D_2 \to \R\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f_1\) is continuous at \(x_0 \in D_1\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f_2\) is continuous at \(f_1(x_0)\text{.}\) Then the composition 2  \(f_2 \circ f_1\) is continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\)
Proof.
Let \((a_n)_n\) be a sequence in \(D_1\) which tends to \(x_0\text{.}\) By definition of the function limit (Definition 5.12), we are required to prove that \(f_2 \circ f_1(a_n)\to f_2 \circ f_1(x_0)\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\) Since \(f_1\) is continuous at \(x_0\) we have \(f_1(a_n)\to f_1(x_0)\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\) The continuity of \(f_2\) at \(f_1(x_0)\) then gives that \(f_2 \brac{f_1(a_n)}\to f_2 \brac{ f_1(x_0)}\) as \(n\to\infty\text{,}\) as required.
