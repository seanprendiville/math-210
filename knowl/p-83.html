Next we prove the "\(\impliedby\)" direction. To do so we prove the contrapositive. Hence, suppose that the negation of (6.3) holds:

\begin{align*} (\exists \eps \gt 0)(\forall \delta \gt 0)\amp(\exists x= x(\delta) \in (a,b))\\ \bigsqbrac{\ 0\lt |x-x_0| \leq \delta\quad \text{and}\quad \abs{\frac{f(x)-f(x_0)}{x-x_0} - y} \gt \eps\ }\text{.} \end{align*}

Taking \(\delta = 1/n\) in the above negation, set \(a_1:= x(1)\text{,}\) \(a_2:=x(1/2)\text{,}\) \(a_3 := x(1/3)\text{,}\) etc. Then \(a_n \in (a,b)\setminus\set{x_0}\text{,}\) \(|a_n - x_0| \leq 1/n\) and

\begin{equation*} \abs{\frac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}{a_n-x_0} - y} \geq \eps\text{.} \end{equation*}

Hence \((a_n)_n\) is a sequence in \((a,b)\setminus\set{x_0}\) with \(a_n \to x_0\) and

\begin{equation*} \frac{f(a_n)-f(x_0)}{a_n-x_0} \not\to y\text{,} \end{equation*}

(as required).
