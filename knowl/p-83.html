Set \(a_n := (-1)^n\) for \(n=1, 2, \dots\text{.}\) Then the partial sums satisfy \(\sum_{n=1}^N a_n = -1\) if \(N\) is odd and \(\sum_{n=1}^N a_n = 0\) if \(N\) is even. It follows that the sequence of partial sums has a subsequence with limit \(-1\) and a subsequence with limit \(0\text{.}\) Therefore the sequence of partial sums cannot tend to a limit, since if a sequence has a limit all its subsequences tend to the same limit (PropositionÂ 3.20). Thus \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^n\) does not exist.
