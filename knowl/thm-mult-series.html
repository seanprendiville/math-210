Theorem 4.32. Multiplying series.

Suppose that the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n\) and \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n\) are both absolutely convergent. Then we have the Cauchy product formula

\begin{equation} \brac{\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n}\brac{\sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \brac{\sum_{r+s = n} a_rb_s}.\tag{4.4} \end{equation}

Furthermore the right-hand side is absolutely convergent.

Here we are using the notation

\begin{equation*} \sum_{r+s = n} a_rb_s := \sum_{r=0}^n a_rb_{n-r} = a_0b_n + a_1b_{n-1} + \dots + a_nb_0\text{.} \end{equation*}
Proof.
