Task 3.29.j.
By the binomial theorem
\begin{equation*} 2^n = (1+1)^n \geq \binom{n}{2} = \trecip{2}n(n-1) \qquad (\forall n \geq 2)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Therefore, for \(n\geq 2\) we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{2^n}{n} \geq \trecip{2}(n-1) \to \infty. \end{equation*}
Hence \(\frac{2^n}{n}\to \infty\) by the (one-sided) sandwich rule.
