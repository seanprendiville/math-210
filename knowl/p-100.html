We first prove the "\(\impliedby\)" direction. Let \((a_n)_n\) be a sequence in \(D\) such that \(a_n \to x_0\) as \(n \to \infty\text{.}\) We are required to prove that \(f(a_n) \to f(x_0)\) as \(n \to \infty\text{,}\) assuming (5.4). Fix \(\eps \gt0\text{.}\) Then by (5.4) there exists \(\delta = \delta(\eps) \gt 0\) such that if \(x \in D\) and \(|x-x_0| \leq \delta\) then \(|f(x)-f(x_0)|\leq \eps\text{.}\) Since \(a_n \to x_0\) there exists \(N = N(\delta)\) such that for all \(n\geq N\) we have \(|a_n - x_0| \leq \delta\text{.}\) Hence if \(n \geq N(\delta)\) then \(|f(a_n)-f(x_0)| \leq \eps\text{.}\) This establishes that \(f(a_n) \to f(x_0)\text{.}\)
