Proof
This follows from the inverse function theorem (Theorem 7.17) with \(f := \exp\text{,}\) since the hypotheses of that theorem are met, namely:

  • \(\exp : \R \to (0,\infty)\) is bijective (this follows from Proposition 8.4).

  • \(\exp^{-1}= \log\) is continuous at every \(x_0 \in (0,\infty)\) (this follows from Proposition 8.8).

  • \(\exp\) is differentiable at every \(x_0\in \R\) with non-zero derivative (this follows from Proposition 8.2).
