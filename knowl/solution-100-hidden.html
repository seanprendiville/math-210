Definition 8.16. Riemann sum.
Let \(a, b\in \R\) with \(a\lt b\text{.}\) Let \(f : [a,b] \to \R\) be a bounded 1  function. Let \(a = x_0 \lt x_1\lt \dots \lt x_N = b\) be a subdivision of \([a,b]\text{.}\) We define the upper Riemann sum of \(f\) with respect to this subdivision by
\begin{equation*} U(f; x_0, \dots, x_N) := \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \sup_{ [x_{n-1}, x_n]} f. \end{equation*}
We define the lower Riemann sum of \(f\) with respect to this subdivision by
\begin{equation*} L(f; x_0, \dots, x_N) := \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \inf_{ [x_{n-1}, x_n]} f. \end{equation*}
Notice that both the upper and lower Riemann sums are finite real numbers, since the fact that \(f\) is bounded implies that
\begin{align} -\infty \amp \lt (b-a)\inf_{[a,b]} f = \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \inf_{ [a,b]} f\notag\\ \amp \leq \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \inf_{ [x_{n-1}, x_n]} f = L(f; x_0, \dots, x_N)\notag\\ \amp\leq \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \sup_{ [x_{n-1}, x_n]} f = U(f; x_0, \dots, x_N)\notag\\ \amp \leq \sum_{n=1}^N (x_n-x_{n-1}) \sup_{ [a,b]} f \notag\\ \amp = (b-a)\sup_{[a,b]} f\lt \infty.\tag{8.6} \end{align}
