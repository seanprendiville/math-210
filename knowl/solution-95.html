Solution 8.11.a.1.

By our conventions for integer powers, if \(a \in (0,\infty)\) and \(n \in \Z\) then the addition rule \(\exp(x)\exp(y) = \exp(x+y)\) gives \(\exp(x)^n = \exp(nx)\text{,}\) hence
\begin{equation*} a^n = \brac{\exp(\log a)}^n = \exp(n\log a). \end{equation*}
We observe that \(\exp\brac{\recip{n} \log a}^n = \exp(\log a) = a\text{.}\) Hence \(\exp\brac{\recip{n} \log a}\) is a positive \(n\)th root of \(a\text{.}\) By uniqueness of positive roots (CorollaryÂ 6.17) we have \(\sqrt[n]{a} = \exp\brac{\recip{n} \log a}\text{.}\)
