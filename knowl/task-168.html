Task 8.21.b.
Define exponentiation to an arbitrary real and the logarithm to an arbitrary base.
Solution.
Definition 8.22. Exponentiation to an arbitrary real.
Given \(a \in (0, \infty)\) and \(x \in \R\) we define
\begin{equation*} a^x := \exp(x\log a). \end{equation*}
We have previously defined \(a^n\) when \(n \in \Z\text{,}\) and (8.7) shows that our two definitions coincide when \(x = n \in \Z\text{.}\) (8.8) also shows that \(a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a} \text{,}\) as we would expect.
Definition 8.23. Logarithm to an arbitrary base.
Given \(a \in (0, \infty)\) with \(a \neq 1\) and \(x \in \R\) we define
\begin{equation*} \log_a(x) := \frac{\log x}{\log a}. \end{equation*}
