Theorem 5.5. Existence of radius of convergence.
Let \((a_n)_{n=0}^\infty\) be a sequence of real numbers. There exists a unique \(R \in [0, \infty]\text{,}\) the radius of convergence, for which both of the following hold.

  1. If \(|x| \lt R\) then the power series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n\) is absolutely convergent.

  2. If \(|x| \gt R\) then the power series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n x^n\) does not converge.

Moreover, the radius of convergence \(R\) is given by the formula
\begin{equation} R= \sup\set{ r \geq 0 : \lim_{n\to \infty} |a_n|r^n = 0}.\tag{5.3} \end{equation}
