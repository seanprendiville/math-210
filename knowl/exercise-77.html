- 9.18. Exam-style self-test question for workshop.

Let \(f :[0,1] \to \R\) be bounded.
(a)
Define what it means to be:
  1. a subdivision of \([0,1]\text{;}\)
  2. the upper and lower Riemann sums of \(f\) with respect to a subdivision;
  3. the upper and lower Riemann integrals of \(f\) and what it means for \(f\) to be Riemann integrable.
Solution.
This is - 9.1.
(b)
Prove that there exist subdivisions \(0=a_0 \lt a_1 \lt \dots \lt a_P = 1\) and \(0=b_0 \lt b_1 \lt \dots \lt b_Q = 1\) such that
\begin{gather*} L(f;[0,1]) - 0.05 \leq L(f;a_0, \dots, a_P),\\ U(f;b_0, \dots, b_Q) \leq U(f;[0,1]) + 0.05. \end{gather*}
Solution.
This is a special case of the argument given at the start of the proof of Proposition 9.8.
(c)
Using the previous part and clearly stating any results from the course which you use, explain how if \(f\) is Riemann integrable, then there exists a subdivision \(0=x_0 \lt \dots \lt x_N = 1\) such that
\begin{equation*} |U(f;x_0, \dots, x_N) - L(f;x_0, \dots, x_N)| \leq 0.1. \end{equation*}
Solution.
This is a special case of the argument given in the proof of Proposition 9.8.
