- 3.31. Exam-style self-test question for workshop.

(a)
Define what it means for a real sequence \((a_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) to satisfy \(a_n \to \infty\) as \(n\to \infty\text{.}\)
Solution.
This is a special case of Task 3.1.a.
(b)
Suppose that \(b_n \to \infty\) as \(n\to \infty\text{.}\) Explain why there exists \(N_0 \in \N\) such that \(b_{N_0}, b_{N_0+1}, b_{N_0+2}, \dots\) are all positive real numbers.
Solution.
This follows from the definition in Task 3.1.a.
(c)
Suppose that \(a_n \to \infty\) and \(b_n\to\infty\) as \(n\to \infty\text{.}\) Prove that the sequence \((a_n+b_n)_{n=1}^\infty\) also satisfies \(a_n+b_n \to \infty\) as \(n\to \infty\text{.}\)
Solution.
This is a special case of Task 3.7.c, using the previous part to establish that \((b_n)_n\) is bounded below if helpful.
