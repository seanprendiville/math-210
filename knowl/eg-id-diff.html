Example 6.26. The identity function is differentiable.
Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\) be given by \(f(x):=x\) for all \(x \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then for \(h\neq 0\) and \(x \in (a,b)\) we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{(x+h)-x}{h} = 1 \to 1 \quad \text{as} \quad h \to 0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(f\) is differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) Moreover \(f' =1\) on \((a,b)\text{.}\)
