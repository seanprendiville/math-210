Theorem 8.11.
Let \(f : (a,b) \to \R\) be a function which is \(n\)-times differentiable and let \(x_0, x \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Then there exists \(x_n\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that
\begin{align} \amp \abs{f(x) - \sqbrac{f(x_0) + (x-x_0)f'(x_0) + \dots + \trecip{(n-1)!} (x-x_0)^{n-1} f^{(n-1)}(x_0)}}\notag\\ \amp \qquad\qquad\qquad \leq |x-x_0|^n|f^{(n)}(x_n)|.\tag{8.2} \end{align}
Proof.
Set
\begin{align*} \amp g(x) := f(x) -\\ \amp \sqbrac{f(x_0) + (x-x_0)f'(x_0) + \trecip{2} (x-x_0)^2 f''(x_0) + \dots + \trecip{(n-1)!} (x-x_0)^{n-1} f^{(n-1)}(x_0)}\text{.} \end{align*}
Our aim is to prove that there exists \(x_n\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that
\begin{equation} \abs{g(x)} \leq |x-x_0|^n \abs{g^{(n)}(x_n)}.\tag{8.3} \end{equation}
We do this by induction on \(n \geq 0\text{,}\) the base case \(n=0\) being automatic. Suppose then that (8.3) holds for \(n-1\text{,}\) so that there exists \(x_{n-1}\) between \(x_0\) and \(x\) such that
\begin{equation*} \abs{g(x)} \leq |x-x_0|^{n-1} \abs{g^{(n-1)}(x_{n-1})}. \end{equation*}
Applying the mean value theorem to \(g^{(n-1)}\text{,}\) there exists \(x_{n}\) between \(x_0\) and \(x_{n-1}\) such that
\begin{equation*} g^{(n-1)}(x_{n-1}) = g^{(n-1)}(x_{0}) + (x_{n-1}-x_0) g^{(n)}(x_n) = (x_{n-1}-x_0) g^{(n)}(x_n). \end{equation*}
Since \(x_{n-1}\) is between \(x_0\) and \(x\text{,}\) we must have that \(x_{n}\) is between \(x_0\) and \(x\) and
\begin{equation*} |x_{n-1}-x_0|\leq |x-x_0|\text{.} \end{equation*}
Therefore
\begin{equation*} \abs{g(x)} \leq |x-x_0|^{n-1} \abs{(x_{n-1}-x_0) g^{(n)}(x_n)} \leq |x-x_0|^{n} \abs{g^{(n)}(x_n)}, \end{equation*}
as required.
