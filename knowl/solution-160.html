Solution 10.6.g.1.
Example 10.13.
Let \(q_1, q_2, \dots\) be an enumeration 4  of the rationals in \([0,1]\text{.}\) The sequence of functions \(f_n := 1_{\set{q_1, \dots, q_n}} : [0,1] \to \R\) converges pointwise to \(f := 1_{\Q \cap [0,1]}\) on \([0,1]\text{.}\) Each \(f_n\) is Riemann integrable on \([0,1]\) by Example 9.19 and linearity of the integral (Theorem 9.13). Moreover \(\int_0^1 f_n = 0\text{.}\) Yet by Proposition 9.26, the function \(f= 1_{\Q \cap [0,1]}\) is not Riemann integrable on \([0,1]\text{.}\) This example shows that, unlike uniform convergence, pointwise convergence is not sufficient to ensure Riemann integrability of the limit.
Proof.
It suffices to show that the \(f_n\) converge pointwise to \(f\) on \([0,1]\text{.}\) Let \(x \in [0,1]\text{.}\) If \(x \notin \Q\) then \(f_n(x) = 0\) for all \(n\) and \(f(x) = 0\text{.}\) Hence \(f_n(x) \to f(x)\) as \(n \to \infty\) when \(x \notin \Q\text{.}\) Next suppose that \(x \in \Q\text{.}\) Then \(x = q_N\) for some \(N \in \N\text{.}\) Hence \(f_n(x) = 1\) for all \(n \geq N\text{.}\) Also \(f(x) = 1\text{.}\) Therefore \(f_n(x) \to f(x)\) as \(n \to \infty\) when \(x \in \Q\text{.}\)
